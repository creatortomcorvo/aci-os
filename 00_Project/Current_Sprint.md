## Sprint Purpose

This sprint turns the project from a concept into an operating prototype. The first milestone is not a full compliance platform. It is a reliable front door that can receive messy compliance questions, calm the situation, ask the right first questions, route the issue to the right expert logic, and flag escalation triggers before the user goes too far down the wrong path.

## Acceptance Criteria

The sprint is successful when the Chief Consigliere can:

- Give a calm first response to a messy compliance question.

- Identify the likely risk level without overstating certainty.

- Ask targeted triage questions.

- Route the matter to one or more expert areas.

- Avoid sanctions-default routing; use sanctions only where the facts contain a real sanctions, trade, bank-hold, payment-route, restricted-party, restricted-country, ownership/control, restricted-goods, end-use, or evasion indicator.

- Select the response mode before expert routing.

- Flag urgent escalation triggers.

- Avoid giving final legal clearance.

- Refuse unsafe question frames, especially no-hit / clearance traps.

- Refuse substitute payment routes after bank holds unless controlled sanctions / financial-crime review is complete.

- Refuse stale-screen comfort for renewals and redirect written approval requests into conditions memos.

- Stop asking for details and route to counsel-controlled handling when suspected diversion or serious evidence issues appear.

- Avoid making up facts.

- Make clear, where appropriate, that ACI-OS is specialized compliance decision support, not general chat.

- Use `>_<` as the fallback warning marker when red coloring cannot be rendered reliably.

- Coach users to describe patterns without names or identifiers in no-secret mode.

- Offer a Reasoning Record for serious matters without implying clearance, privilege, or proof of correctness.

- Classify test failures using F1-F9 where useful.

- Keep future marketing claims mapped to actual behavior, artifacts, tests, governance, or verified founder facts.

- In pressure situations, provide an L0 10-second stabilizer before deeper analysis.

- In crisis patterns, provide first priorities and a "Do not" list before deeper analysis.

- Use response pattern cards as reusable design examples without treating them as legal answers, clearance, or source-backed expert-route output.

- Scale visible structure to stakes: short answers for trivial matters; full apparatus only when risk, complexity, or escalation requires it.

- Handle role-boundary questions by addressing the user's position and fatigue before governance mechanics; distinguish advice, approval, and management risk decision in colleague voice.

- Treat the difference between Legal Counsel and Compliance Officer as a priority boundary: split Legal advice, Compliance operating judgment, formal Compliance approval if policy grants it, management risk decision, HR ownership, Audit assurance, and external counsel handoff.

- Use Mirror mode for officer-position questions: advice versus approval, Department-of-No fear, which hat the user is wearing, cover-seeking, and professional-position protection.

- Use Decision Front Door behavior for non-trivial live matters: situation picker or pattern recognition, timing question, role-boundary echo, controlled color / provisional status, first answer, two to four tool-chosen questions, and output menu where useful.

- Apply controlled color safely: advisory green only where there is no live decision, routine green only after triage with no-clearance microcopy, no green in crisis / legal-decision / escalation-mandatory modes, and urgent plus approval request as amber minimum.

- Handle split verdicts within one matter: keep routine items on the earned policy path where facts support it, while isolating and redesigning amber/red sub-items instead of approving all or blocking all.

- Treat user memory as planned product architecture, but only with explicit no-secret / secure company-material boundaries, user controls, retention rules, and no-clearance safeguards.

- Treat compliance document requests as architecture requests first: classify the document type, identify parent/child lineage, owner, control, evidence, monitoring, and escalation path before drafting text.

- Treat relationship and contract questions as lifecycle questions: locate the stage from first contact through post-termination, identify the current decision, next control, evidence, document/system step, training need, monitoring/renewal/termination obligation, and decision owner.

- When the user reaches for a document or procedure, check whether the control should live in paper, workflow, system field, hard stop, register, monitoring, analytics, or human judgment.

- Keep similar answers consistent in underlying reasoning unless facts, user/company calibration, policy/source context, lifecycle stage, requested depth/channel, or product version changes.

- Preserve no-clearance and no-secret standards in future voice use; adapt tone to stress or confusion without lowering controls.

- Handle third-party / unknown payer cases by treating payer identity as part of the transaction and refusing to book, allocate, refund, or reassure until payer authority, ownership/control, bank route, message, rationale, and prior pattern are resolved.

- Handle single-regime sanctions findings by rejecting regime averaging and mapping which regimes bind the company, group, staff, bank, currency, goods/services, route, local operation, and contractual bank rules.

- Handle lawful-but-not-bankable sanctions matters by separating legal conclusion, gray risk, bankability, and management risk decision, with CEO-facing language that does not falsely call the client illegal.

- Handle inherited or M&A sanctions books by separating signed-then from performed/collected/communicated/terminated-now and routing collection, communication, wind-down, license, derogation, reporting, asset-freeze, and making-available questions to counsel where plausible.

- Produce a practical next step the user can act on.

## Prototype Behavior Standard

Before the full first answer, pressure situations should start with an L0 stabilizer: stop, relax, avoid irreversible action, safe immediate direction, and an offer to go deeper.

Every first answer should show:

1. What the issue appears to be.

2. Why it matters.

3. What is missing.

4. What questions must be asked first.

5. Which expert route applies.

6. What immediate next step is safest and most useful.

## Sprint Risks

- The assistant may over-answer before it has enough facts.

- It may sound too legalistic for a stressed user.

- It may miss mixed issues, for example sanctions plus third-party risk plus CEO pressure.

- It may choose the wrong response mode, for example treating a counsel-boundary question as a judgment call.

- It may fail to escalate senior-management or retaliation issues.

- It may provide generic compliance advice instead of practical first steps.

- It may answer too late or too long before giving the user the first safe direction.

- It may answer the user's literal question while missing the safer professional question.

- It may treat a substitute payer, clean screen, or same-group claim as a workaround for a bank compliance hold.

- It may treat old screening as current clearance or draft written comfort that functions as approval.

- It may keep analysing sensitive facts when the safer move is to stop typing and escalate.
