# Competitive Landscape And Product Lessons v0.1

Status: initial external scan. Public product pages only. Sources accessed 2026-07-06.

Purpose: understand how adjacent GRC, compliance, risk-data, and professional-AI products are structured, and decide what ACI-OS should learn without drifting into the wrong product category.

## Bottom Line

The market is not missing another compliance database, hotline, policy tool, screening engine, or GRC workflow suite.

The gap ACI-OS can own is the **judgment interface**: the layer that helps an under-supported compliance leader understand the situation, choose the right response mode, ask the missing questions, use existing systems correctly, record the decision, and communicate with business, Legal, management, providers, and the board.

## Scope And Limits

Covered in this first scan:

- GRC / ethics / compliance suites.
- Third-party, sanctions, and risk-intelligence providers.
- Board / reporting / assurance platforms.
- Legal and professional AI assistants.

Not covered yet:

- Pricing.
- Security questionnaires or trust-center evidence.
- User reviews and implementation pain.
- Procurement comparison.
- Deep feature-by-feature scoring.
- Private demos.

## Competitor / Adjacent Product Observations

### NAVEX One

Public positioning: unified AI-powered GRC platform covering risk, regulations, training, whistleblowing, policies, disclosures, third-party risk, analytics, benchmarking, and board-ready dashboards.

What to learn:

- Shared data foundation matters. Disconnected tools create weak reporting and missed patterns.
- Strong product narrative: connect risk -> policy/control updates -> training -> repeat-issue reduction.
- Start with one urgent module and scale later is a useful adoption model.

ACI-OS implication:

- ACI-OS should **not** build a NAVEX clone.
- ACI-OS should be able to say: "This belongs in your hotline/case system", "This belongs in your policy workflow", "This belongs in your third-party tool", and then help draft the right entry.

Verdict: **ACT** for connected-evidence thinking. **PARK** for full suite/workflow build.

### Diligent One

Public positioning: board and GRC platform centralizing governance, risk, compliance, audit, third-party risk, policy, due diligence, speak-up, education, board reporting, market intelligence, and integrations with many data providers.

What to learn:

- Board language is a product feature. Diligent sells clarity to leadership, not only internal compliance administration.
- Integration with third-party data sources is a structural advantage.
- Board-facing templates and dashboards matter because compliance work must travel upward.

ACI-OS implication:

- ACI-OS should become excellent at converting messy compliance issues into **board / CEO / GC-ready decision language**.
- Future product should export concise decision records, board lines, and "what management must decide" blocks.

Verdict: **ACT** for board-facing clarity and integration logic. **PARK** for board portal / dashboard build.

### OneTrust

Public positioning: platform for responsible data use with privacy automation, AI governance, tech risk/compliance, and third-party management. Third-party lifecycle includes intake, risk assessment, mitigation, monitoring, reporting, onboarding, and offboarding.

What to learn:

- Lifecycle language is becoming market standard.
- AI governance and data governance are close neighbors to compliance operations.
- Mature buyers expect privacy/security/data-risk architecture, not only good answers.

ACI-OS implication:

- The user-memory design must separate no-secret mode from secure company-material mode.
- Third-party answers should remain lifecycle-based: first contact, due diligence, safeguards, monitoring, renewal, exit, afterlife.

Verdict: **ACT** for lifecycle framing and secure-memory discipline. **PARK** for AI governance product route.

### GAN Integrity

Public positioning: compliance and third-party risk software covering third-party risk, conflicts, gifts and entertainment, ABAC, supply chain due diligence, policy management, incident management, analytics, dashboards, and integrations.

What to learn:

- GAN speaks directly to the small-team reality: move off spreadsheets and email into a system of record.
- It frames low-risk automation correctly: automation handles low-risk gifts and entertainment so humans can focus on judgment cases.
- Third-party management is positioned as continuous, not annual.

ACI-OS implication:

- ACI-OS should keep the "human judgment reserved for judgment cases" principle.
- Low-risk answers should be short, conditional, and procedural; high-risk matters should trigger full apparatus.
- Third-party monitoring should be treated as a relationship over time, not a one-time screen.

Verdict: **ACT** for proportionality and small-team workflow pain. **PARK** full TPRM system-of-record functionality.

### EQS Group

Public positioning: compliance, privacy, ESG, and investor-relations software. Compliance cockpit includes whistleblowing/case management, third parties, approvals, policies, insights; strong European regulatory framing.

What to learn:

- European regulatory posture is commercially important.
- "Can we prove that?" is a powerful product idea: audit-ready record, not just advice.
- Approvals/disclosures need automated routing and complete logs.

ACI-OS implication:

- ACI-OS should keep an explicit Europe-first voice where relevant.
- Every serious answer should help create a defensible record: what was known, unknown, advised, escalated, and decided.
- ACI-OS should not become the whistleblowing channel or case system.

Verdict: **ACT** for European cockpit/evidence orientation. **PARK** full case-management / approval workflow.

### ServiceNow IRM

Public positioning: integrated risk management for risk-informed decisions, workflows, AI, data, third-party risk, privacy, security, and enterprise process automation.

What to learn:

- At enterprise scale, risk and compliance live inside workflow automation, not in standalone advice.
- User value comes from connecting people, processes, systems, and data.

ACI-OS implication:

- Future ACI-OS should integrate with workflow systems where companies already work.
- Current ACI-OS should remain the reasoning layer that tells the user which workflow, owner, evidence, and next step matter.

Verdict: **PARK** as future integration reference.

### LSEG Risk Intelligence / World-Check

Public positioning: risk intelligence suite for sanctions, PEPs, adverse media, enforcement actions, due diligence, identity verification, onboarding, and account verification, with API-first and real-time data products.

What to learn:

- High-quality risk data is a different product from compliance judgment.
- API-first screening and payment/onboarding embedding are the direction of travel.
- Screening produces signals; it does not decide what the company should do.

ACI-OS implication:

- Never compete with World-Check, Dow Jones, LexisNexis, ComplyAdvantage, Moody's, etc. as a data provider.
- ACI-OS should ask: what list/source was checked, what version/date, what regimes bind us, what ownership/control gap remains, what payment/relationship action is next?

Verdict: **ACT** for provider-router logic. **DISCARD** any idea of building proprietary sanctions data.

### Workiva

Public positioning: AI-powered platform for finance, risk, sustainability, governed data, traceable assurance, reporting, controls, audit, policies, and risk management.

What to learn:

- Traceability and version control are trust features.
- Board and external reporting require evidence chains, not just text.
- Secure AI for confidential work is becoming expected.

ACI-OS implication:

- Decision records should carry version, date, sources, user-provided facts, assumptions, gaps, and owner.
- Secure company-material mode is not optional in mature product design.

Verdict: **ACT** for traceable record design. **PARK** reporting-platform functionality.

### Lexis+ With Protege / Thomson Reuters CoCounsel Family / Harvey

Public positioning: professional AI for legal work, using trusted content, source-grounded research, drafting, secure document spaces, workflows, agents, and organization knowledge.

What to learn:

- Users trust professional AI more when it is grounded in trusted sources and firm/company knowledge.
- Workflow libraries and reusable expert skills matter.
- Secure document vaults / knowledge spaces are becoming standard for professional work.
- "Approve the approach before multi-step agents act" is an important control pattern.

ACI-OS implication:

- ACI-OS should keep source footers, source labels, and the distinction between official sources and practitioner method.
- The product should build reusable pattern cards and routes, not one giant prompt.
- The agent should not perform uncontrolled actions; it should propose and document the next step.

Verdict: **ACT** for source-grounding, reusable workflows, and controlled agent design. **PARK** end-to-end autonomous agents.

## What ACI-OS Should Copy

1. **Connected record logic**: answers should create a matter/decision record, not just a chat reply.
2. **Lifecycle thinking**: third parties, investigations, training, policies, and board reporting should be handled over time.
3. **Board-ready output**: translate risk into decisions, asks, ownership, and evidence.
4. **Source-grounding**: official sources, links, and practitioner method must be labeled separately.
5. **Secure knowledge modes**: no-secret mode and company-material mode must be visibly different.
6. **Workflow handoff**: tell the user which company system/provider should be used next.
7. **Proportional automation**: low-risk routine matters can be short; serious matters get full apparatus.
8. **Integration later**: external screening, hotline, policy, GRC, and reporting systems are future connectors, not replacements.

## What ACI-OS Should Not Copy

1. A full GRC suite before the methodology is stable.
2. A proprietary sanctions or due-diligence database.
3. A whistleblowing hotline or investigation case-management system.
4. Board dashboards before the decision-record model is proven.
5. "AI clears this" language.
6. Generic legal-research positioning.
7. Heavy enterprise workflow before the solo compliance-officer experience is excellent.

## ACI-OS Differentiation

The competitor market is strong on systems, data, records, dashboards, and workflows.

ACI-OS should be strongest where those tools are weak:

- **First 30 seconds**: calm, short, correct first move.
- **Question front door**: ask only the missing facts that change the answer.
- **Role clarity**: advice, recommendation, Compliance approval, Legal decision, management risk decision, escalation mandatory.
- **No-clearance discipline**: no-hit does not equal clearance.
- **Pressure recognition**: CEO/CFO/business pressure is part of the fact pattern.
- **Founder method**: practical compliance survival knowledge, labeled as `[practitioner method - Kruk]`.
- **Upward management**: pre-write mandates, escalation lines, and decision menus for leaders.
- **Judgment under uncertainty**: confirmed facts, assumptions, inferences, gaps, required actions.
- **Proportional rendering**: the format itself signals seriousness.
- **Decision memory**: what was asked, what was missing, who owned the decision, what became stale.

## Product Implications For Current Build

ACT now:

- Keep improving the GPT behavior layer: short first answer, questions when broad or unclear, sources with links, visual hierarchy, no self-reference.
- Maintain the decision journal and failure log as the evidence base.
- Add a provider/system-router principle: when the answer depends on a tool or provider, say which one and why.
- Build a simple "matter card" concept in the methodology: question, mode, role label, facts, gaps, next action, owner, source basis, date/staleness.
- Keep strengthening founder-method files: upward management, organization/person-first, risk assessment after joining, third-party lifecycle, training design.

PARK:

- Full user interface.
- API integrations.
- Full GRC workflows.
- Automated reminders/actions from GPT into Codex.
- Secure company-material mode architecture.
- Board dashboard.
- Enterprise pilot.

DISCARD:

- Replacing external due diligence/screening providers.
- Replacing hotline/speak-up systems.
- Replacing legal counsel.
- Building a broad generic AI compliance chatbot without method.
