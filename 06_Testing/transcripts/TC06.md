No — do not proceed on that basis.

Treat this as a sanctions ownership/control and “making available” risk, not a clean company-screening issue.



Provisional status: Red / hold.

Role label: Escalation mandatory — sanctions/legal review before contracting, onboarding, work orders, payment, shipment, or giving access.



The legal-form point does not solve the risk. If the managing director is personally listed, the key question is whether he owns, controls, directs, benefits from, signs for, or can cause the company to receive funds or economic resources. EU guidance treats asset-freeze and “making available” restrictions as extending beyond the listed name where a listed person owns or controls a non-listed entity; the Commission states that funds or economic resources made available to an owned/controlled non-listed entity may be presumed to reach or benefit the listed person.



Your screening result is therefore not “company clean = OK.” The sanctions route requires you to keep list status, ownership/control, management role, transaction chain, and legal conclusion separate; a clean screen on the contracting entity does not clear the transaction or the relationship. The internal decision-front-door logic also treats this as a live matter where green cannot be assumed and escalation is required before action.



Immediate next steps:



Pause the engagement: no contract signature, purchase order, shipment handover, subcontracting instruction, system access, or payment.

Validate the match: confirm the managing director’s identity against the EU listing using identifiers, not just name similarity.

Map ownership/control: shareholding, voting rights, board appointment rights, signatory powers, bank mandates, operational authority, ability to direct the logistics work, and whether he can benefit economically.

Map the transaction chain: routes, goods/services, vehicles/vessels/carriers, banks, currency, countries, end customers, and any sanctioned-country nexus.

Escalate to sanctions counsel / designated sanctions owner for the legal conclusion and any possible lawful pathway or required refusal/exit.



The practical compliance position is:



“We cannot proceed based only on the company having no direct hit. A listed managing director creates an unresolved sanctions-control and benefit risk. The engagement is on hold pending identity confirmation, ownership/control review, transaction-chain review, and sanctions/legal advice.”



A file note could read:



“Subcontractor entity had no direct screening hit, but managing director showed a potential/confirmed EU sanctions listing. Engagement paused. No clearance given. Referred for sanctions/legal review of identity, ownership/control, benefit, authority, transaction chain, and applicable regimes as of 4 July 2026.”



Do not let the business quote this as “Compliance was fine because the company had no hit.”
