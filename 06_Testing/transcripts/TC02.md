QUESTION

Quick one — we screened our Belarusian logistics supplier six months ago, all clear, we even documented it. Contract auto-renews tomorrow. Nothing to do, right?



ANSWER

No — do not treat the six-month-old screen as enough for tomorrow’s auto-renewal.



Immediate direction: pause the renewal step if you can, or at least put it under a same-day current sanctions / ownership / transaction-chain refresh before the contract rolls. A documented “no hit” from six months ago is evidence of diligence then, not continuing clearance now.



Role label: this is Compliance advice / escalation screening, not Compliance approval or clearance.



For a Belarusian logistics supplier, I would treat this as amber: possible to renew only after the current checks are refreshed and recorded. The issue is not that the old review was bad; it is that sanctions exposure can change across lists, ownership/control, banks, routes, goods, services, and logistics actors. The sanctions route specifically treats dated screening used for renewal or contract extension as a pause-and-refresh pattern, not a “nothing to do” pattern.



Minimum same-day refresh before renewal:



Re-screen the supplier now against the applicable regimes for your group, banks, currency, routes, and operations.

Refresh ownership/control, not just the legal entity name.

Check directors/signatories or controlling persons if relevant to your process.

Confirm routes, countries, banks, goods/services, subcontractors, carriers, and any Belarus/Russia nexus.

Review the contract sanctions clause: termination, suspension, audit/info rights, no circumvention, no restricted-party involvement, and change-notification duty.

Document who owns the renewal decision if any residual risk remains.



Do not write: “screened, all clear, nothing further required.”

Better file line:



“Prior screening dated \[date] is treated as historical diligence only. Given renewal on \[date] and Belarus-related sanctions exposure, Compliance requires current screening, ownership/control refresh, route/bank/service review, and sanctions-clause check before renewal proceeds. No clearance is given on the basis of the stale screen alone.”



If the business says the auto-renewal cannot technically be stopped, send a short same-day note preserving the position: renewal is not being positively approved by Compliance until the refresh is complete, and any performance/payment/shipment activity should remain subject to the refreshed review.
