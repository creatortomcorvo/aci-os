QUESTION



Our bank froze a payment coming in from our distributor in Central Asia — compliance hold, no explanation yet. CFO says the distributor offered to just route it through their trading company in Dubai instead, different name, same group. CEO wants to know in 15 minutes if we can accept that. Money is significant. Can we?



ANSWER

Situation understood. Do not accept, invite, or signal acceptance of the Dubai trading-company route today.

This is not a payment-ops workaround; it is a bank-hold plus substitute-route pattern, which should be treated as a sanctions / financial-crime evasion-risk event until reviewed.



Answer to CEO in 15 minutes:



“I cannot approve accepting a substitute payment in 15 minutes. The original payment is under a bank compliance hold, and routing through a different group entity could create sanctions or financial-crime evasion risk for us. We need the bank’s written basis for the hold and a documented review of the substitute entity, ownership/control, payment chain, bank route, and commercial rationale before we accept any funds.”



Risk read: Red / escalation mandatory.

The money being significant and the CEO pressure matter, but they do not reduce the control requirement. Urgent plus approval request is at least amber; here the bank hold plus reroute proposal makes it red until controlled.



Do not do these now:



Do not accept funds from the Dubai entity.

Do not tell the distributor “yes,” “probably okay,” or “send it that way.”

Do not change invoice wording, payer details, contract party, or payment instructions to fit the workaround.

Do not let Finance book, allocate, net, refund, or manually match any substitute payment without review.

Do not debate facts with the distributor yet; avoid tipping them into shaping a cleaner story.



Immediate next steps:



Ask the bank, in writing, for the basis of the compliance hold, expected timing, and whether it relates to sanctions, AML, ownership/control, country, currency, bank route, or payment-message concerns.

Open an internal controlled case with Legal / sanctions / financial-crime compliance.

Preserve the bank notice, original payment details, contract, invoices, distributor due diligence, ownership information, and the distributor’s reroute proposal.

Map the full payment chain: original payer, proposed Dubai payer, ownership/control, same-group evidence, banks, currencies, countries, invoice references, and business rationale.

Keep the CEO/CFO answer framed as “not today / not without review,” not as a final rejection of the relationship.



Sharp triage questions for the internal case, not for open chat with names:



Is the bank hold explicitly sanctions-related, AML-related, or unexplained?

Are the distributor and Dubai trading company under common ownership/control, and can that be independently verified?

Are any Central Asia, Russia, Iran, Belarus, sanctioned-country, military/end-use, dual-use, state-owned, or politically exposed links present?

Would the Dubai company be paying under the contract, or would it be an unrelated third-party payer?



Record line for your file:



“Inbound payment placed on bank compliance hold; counterparty proposed substitute payment via different group entity in Dubai. Compliance advised no acceptance or external comfort pending written bank basis and sanctions/financial-crime review of payment chain, ownership/control, bank route, and commercial rationale. Decision escalated to Legal/sanctions/financial-crime owner; management informed this is not cleared for acceptance today.
