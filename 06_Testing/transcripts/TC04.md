Situation understood. Treat this as serious suspected diversion / dual-use end-use risk. Your first move is not to investigate more alone; it is to preserve and escalate under controlled handling.



Do not delete, edit, annotate, forward around, reorganise, or “clean up” the emails. Do not contact the reseller or suspected end-user. Do not type names, routes, product details, email contents, or live facts into this chat.



Status: Red — stop / preserve / escalate.

Role label: escalation mandatory / counsel-controlled handling. This is no longer a routine compliance review. It may involve sanctions, export controls, diversion, false end-use representations, books-and-records issues, and possible regulatory exposure.



What to do first:



Pause any affected pending shipment, release, service, order acceptance, renewal, or payment step if you have authority to do so through normal internal controls. If you do not have authority, escalate immediately to someone who does.

Preserve passively. Keep the emails and related records where they are. Do not forward them broadly. Do not mark them up. Do not start interviewing people. Do not ask the reseller “where are these really going?”

Notify the designated internal owner now: usually the GC / Legal, sanctions/export-control lead, investigation lead, or crisis escalation channel. If Legal may be implicated or conflicted, escalate according to your policy to independent counsel, board/audit committee channel, or the designated speak-up/investigation owner.

Ask Legal to control the next step. Counsel should decide preservation instructions, privilege-intended review structure, shipment hold, employee access limits, external counsel/export-control specialist involvement, and whether any regulator, licensing, customs, or disclosure question arises.

Create a minimal private note for yourself, not a narrative accusation:

“On \[date/time], I identified emails suggesting possible reseller diversion / dual-use end-use concern involving subsidiary shipments. I have not contacted external parties or continued fact-gathering. I preserved records in place and escalated to \[role/channel] for counsel-controlled review.”



Only three immediate triage questions matter right now, and answer them internally or to counsel, not here with identifiers:



Is any shipment, release, service access, or payment pending in hours or days?

Are the components plausibly dual-use, controlled, military-linked, or subject to export/sanctions restrictions?

Is there any risk that emails, shipping records, ERP records, or chat messages could be deleted, altered, or overwritten?



If the answer to any of those is yes or “don’t know,” escalate as urgent and ask for a formal hold/pause instruction today.
