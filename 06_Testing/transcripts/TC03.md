Thought for 38s



I would not draft an “approved” confirmation on these facts.



This is an amber / pause-before-approval third-party onboarding pattern: board-member recommendation, ministry connections, and clean screening are not enough. Clean screening only tells you something about the screening result; it does not resolve anti-bribery, influence, conflict-of-interest, public-sector, third-party, fee, deliverables, or sanctions-adjacent exposure. The right output is a conditions / risk memo, not a Compliance approval note.



You can send this instead:



Subject: Compliance review status – proposed Central Asia commercial agent



Compliance cannot provide an approval confirmation at this stage based only on screening results.



The proposed engagement requires enhanced third-party review before any appointment, commitment, payment, introduction, or use of the agent. The current file includes risk factors that need to be resolved and documented, including the agent’s government / ministry connections, the source of the board-member recommendation, the proposed scope of services, fee structure, beneficial ownership, conflicts of interest, and any public-official or state-owned-enterprise touchpoints.



Sales should not describe the agent as “Compliance approved” or proceed on that basis.



Compliance can complete its risk view once the following are provided:



proposed scope of work and concrete deliverables;

fee / commission basis, success-fee elements, payment route, and recipient bank account;

countries, customers, end users, tenders, ministries, SOEs, or public officials involved;

ownership / control information and management details for the agent;

explanation of the board-member connection and any actual or perceived conflict of interest;

proposed contract terms, including anti-bribery, sanctions, audit, termination, books-and-records, and no-subagent provisions.



Pending completion of that review, Compliance’s position is: no appointment or external use of the agent yet; continue due diligence and route any exception request through the appropriate approval owner under company policy.



This is a Compliance risk position, not a legal clearance or commercial go / no-go decision. If management wishes to proceed despite residual risk after review, that should be recorded as a management risk decision through the proper governance channel.



For your file, I would record:



“Sales requested written Compliance approval for proposed Central Asia commercial agent. Compliance declined approval wording and required enhanced third-party due diligence due to board-member referral, ministry connections, and public-sector influence risk. Screening result noted but not treated as clearance. Decision ownership and conditions communicated – 4 July 2026.”



The misquote risk here is real: if you write “approved,” the sentence will later become “Compliance was fine with it.” Keep the written line to risk position + required conditions + decision owner, not approval.
