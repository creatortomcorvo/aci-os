QUESTION:



Ownership check on a potential JV partner: the chain goes through two holding companies and stops — last layer is a nominee structure we can't see through. Also found that a person who owned the group \~20 years ago was on the US list back then; he was delisted years ago and supposedly exited. Everything current screens clean. Good enough?



ANSWER



Not enough for a positive path yet. The live issue is not the old listing by itself; it is current opacity plus unresolved control/benefit evidence.



Thought for 1m 3s



Not good enough for a positive JV recommendation yet. I would mark this Amber / High: pause JV approval, signing, funding, or onboarding until the last ownership/control layer is resolved or formally risk-dealt with by the right owner.



The issue is not “a person was listed 20 years ago, so the group is blocked.” The issue is: you cannot currently see through the ownership/control chain, and the historical sanctions fact makes that opacity materially more serious. A current no-hit screen does not answer who owns, controls, benefits from, or can influence the JV partner.



Current official guidance supports that distinction. OFAC’s 50 Percent Rule treats entities as blocked where blocked persons directly or indirectly own 50% or more in aggregate, and OFAC also urges caution where a blocked person may have significant ownership below 50% or may control the entity by other means. UK OFSI guidance similarly expects firms to consider whether an entity is owned or controlled by a designated person, even where the entity itself is not separately listed. SECO also states that its sanctions search tool shows directly sanctioned persons/entities and does not provide ownership or control information, which is exactly the gap here. This is also consistent with the Chief Consigliere rule that screening is one control, not clearance, and that opaque ownership plus historical designation requires current UBO/control resolution before any positive path.



Compliance position



You can say:



“Current list screening has not identified a listed party on the information available, but the review is not complete. The ownership chain stops at a nominee layer, and there is a historical sanctions-control concern. Compliance cannot support a positive JV recommendation until current beneficial ownership, control, economic benefit, and exit evidence are resolved and documented.”



Do not write “cleared,” “approved,” “no sanctions issue,” or “good to proceed.”



What you need before any positive path



Minimum conditions:



Current UBO/control evidence through the nominee layer, not just a nominee declaration.

Nominee rationale: why the structure exists, who appointed the nominee, who can replace them, voting instructions, side letters, trusts, powers of attorney, shareholder agreements, call options, veto rights, board appointment rights, financing arrangements, and profit entitlement.

Historical exit evidence for the formerly listed owner: sale documents, dates, consideration/payment trail, buyer identity, absence of retained rights, absence of debt/pledges/options, and confirmation he has no present control, benefit, board influence, signatory authority, advisory role, family/close-associate holding, or funding link.

Current screening and adverse-media refresh on disclosed UBOs, controllers, directors, signatories, major lenders, and any trust/nominee principals.

Regime mapping: which sanctions regimes matter for your group, JV entity, banks, currency, staff, technology/services, governing law, and counterparties.

The decision owner



This is not a Compliance “yes/no” based on clean screens. It separates like this:



Legal / sanctions counsel should own the legal assessment of sanctions applicability, control standards, and whether the evidence is sufficient under applicable regimes.



Compliance owns the risk view: current ownership is unresolved, nominee opacity is a control gap, historical designation is a serious contextual red flag, and clean screening is insufficient.



Management owns the commercial risk decision only after Legal and Compliance have defined the residual risk and conditions. Management cannot risk-accept an unresolved sanctions prohibition.



Four triage questions

Is there any imminent signature, funding, bank account opening, asset transfer, IP/license sharing, or operational start?

Which regimes could bind you: Switzerland, EU, UK, US, UN, local law, USD clearing, US persons, EU/UK banks, or group policy?

What exactly is the nominee layer: nominee shareholder, trust, foundation, bearer-like arrangement, professional trustee, or undisclosed private arrangement?

What evidence do you have that the formerly listed person exited fully, and who currently benefits economically from the group?

File note



“Current screens showed no identified listing based on disclosed information, but ownership/control remains unresolved because the chain stops at a nominee structure. Historical designation of a former owner increases the need for current UBO/control and exit evidence. Compliance position: no positive JV recommendation or clearance language until ownership/control, benefit, nominee rationale, and sanctions-counsel review are documented.”



My bottom line: not disqualified automatically, but not good enough yet.
