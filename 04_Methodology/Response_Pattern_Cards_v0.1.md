## Purpose

The cards give ACI-OS repeatable answer shapes for common high-pressure compliance moments.

They should help the router decide:

1. Which response mode applies.
2. Whether a 10-second L0 stabilizer is needed.
3. What the first safe direction is.
4. What not to do.
5. Which questions change the answer.
6. When to stop analysis and escalate.

They do not clear a payment, transaction, person, hospitality item, investigation step, disclosure decision, or legal position.

## Mode Legend

| Mode | Name | Use |
| ---: | --- | --- |
| M1 | Crisis | Immediate operational pressure or authority contact. Use L0 / Speed 1 first. |
| M2 | Judgment Call | Practical compliance decision under business pressure. Use pattern recognition, conditions, and if-thens. |
| M3 | Counsel-Boundary | Jurisdiction-specific legal question. Convert into a counsel briefing and safe interim controls. |
| M4 | Program Self-Assessment | Effectiveness, remediation, maturity, resourcing, or policy defensibility question. |
| M5 | Escalation-Mandatory | Disclosure, senior-executive, criminal, serious evidence, or conflicted-process issue. Structured handoff is the product. |

## Use Rules

- Choose mode before expert route.
- If modes compete, choose the more protective mode first.
- In crisis patterns, provide L0 first: stop, avoid irreversible action, safe immediate direction, then controlled triage.
- In judgment calls, do not produce clearance. Produce conditions, gaps, and decision-record wording.
- In counsel-boundary patterns, do not answer the legal question. Prepare the counsel conversation.
- In escalation-mandatory patterns, do not invite sensitive facts in no-secret mode.
- Use the 7-element backbone in 00_Project/PROJECT_DISCIPLINE.md. Add 12-element Annex A calibration where useful.

## Cards

### Card 01 - Facilitation Demand / Extortion

Mode: M1 Crisis, with possible M5 if safety, official misconduct, or books-and-records exposure is serious.

Pattern: an official demands a payment to release goods, clear customs, allow access, or perform an official function.

Opening move: Official payment demand. Stop for a moment. Do not pay or negotiate yet unless someone is in genuine danger. If there is a safety risk, safety comes first and the record must be created immediately afterwards.

Direction: separate a real safety exception from commercial pressure. If life, liberty, or physical safety is at risk, prioritize safety, record what happened, report internally the same day, and treat the accounting record as part of the response. If it is commercial delay or inconvenience, do not pay. Ask for the lawful basis or official fee in writing, use official channels, and escalate locally.

Do-not list:

- Do not negotiate the amount.
- Do not describe it internally as a small informal fee.
- Do not let the business book it creatively.
- Do not hide a safety-driven payment; that makes the second problem worse.

Decisive questions:

- Is anyone's safety, liberty, or physical security at risk right now?
- What exactly is being withheld?
- Is there a lawful express process or official fee with receipt?
- Has this checkpoint, office, or individual made similar demands before?

If-then triggers:

- If safety is at risk, protect people first, document immediately, report internally same day, and involve Legal / external counsel.
- If only timing or commercial pressure is at stake, no payment and escalate through official or management channels.
- If a pattern exists, treat it as a control and escalation issue, not a one-off exception.

Trap named: urgency is the extortionist's product.

Expert routes: ABAC; Investigations; Speak-Up; Legal / external counsel; Finance controls.

Program stress points: Governance and mandate; Standards and policies; Procedures and controls; Speak-up and reporting; Investigations and response; Remediation and continuous improvement.

### Card 02 - Dawn Raid / Regulator Contact

Mode: M1 Crisis, often with M5 if enforcement, criminal, or serious evidence risk appears.

Pattern: regulator, prosecutor, competition authority, police, or other authority contacts the business or arrives on site.

Opening move: Regulator contact. Pause the room. Preserve everything. One coordinator speaks for the company until counsel confirms the process.

Direction: cooperate, preserve, and narrow the channel. Read and copy the authority request or warrant. Identify the single coordinator. Call internal and external counsel before substantive engagement where possible. Log what is asked, what is taken, who speaks, and when.

Do-not list:

- Do not delete, move, tidy, rename, or clean up files.
- Do not speculate or volunteer explanations beyond what is asked.
- Do not let multiple employees improvise responses.
- Do not obstruct the authority; objections belong to counsel through proper channels.

Decisive questions:

- What does the request, warrant, or authority mandate actually cover?
- Who is the single point of contact?
- Has Legal / external counsel been contacted?
- Are employees being asked for immediate interviews?

If-then triggers:

- If employees are being interviewed, counsel should confirm local rights and process.
- If the authority appears to exceed scope, note it calmly and let counsel handle the objection.
- If data or documents may be altered by normal business activity, preserve immediately.

Trap named: over-cooperation that creates uncontrolled records.

Expert routes: Dawn Raid / Regulator Contact; Investigations; Legal / external counsel; Data / privacy; Governance.

Program stress points: Governance and mandate; Procedures and controls; Training and communication; Investigations and response; Monitoring, auditing, and testing.

### Card 03 - Hospitality Package For Officials

Mode: M2 Judgment Call.

Pattern: VIP tickets, travel, hotel, entertainment, or event hospitality for a government, federation, customer, or other decision-maker.

Opening move: Hospitality for a decision-maker. The question is not the ticket alone; it is timing, influence, total value, and transparency.

Direction: break the issue into decision power, pending decisions, proportionality, business purpose, and transparency. The answer is conditional, not item-based.

Do-not list:

- Do not evaluate the package as "just tickets."
- Do not rely on industry custom.
- Do not approve verbally what would look weak in a written file.
- Do not ignore the recipient's own employer or public-sector rules.

Decisive questions:

- What decision affecting the company can this person influence in the next 12 months?
- Is any tender, renewal, permit, inspection, dispute, or negotiation live?
- Would the recipient's employer or authority approve the invitation if asked directly?
- Is there a genuine business program, or only entertainment?

If-then triggers:

- If a tender, renewal, or official decision is live, default to no or defer until the decision is closed.
- If the recipient is a public official, the recipient-side rules may be the ceiling.
- If a third party pays or hosts, run third-party and books-and-records checks as well.

Trap named: industry-norm bias.

Expert routes: ABAC; Third Party Due Diligence if sponsored by another party; Governance / Tone at the Top; Business Expectations.

Program stress points: Risk assessment; Standards and policies; Procedures and controls; Training and communication; Third-party risk management.

### Card 04 - Travel For Officials

Mode: M2 Judgment Call.

Pattern: company pays flights, hotels, per diems, transport, or itinerary costs for officials or customer representatives attending an event, inspection, or presentation.

Opening move: Paying for an official journey can be legitimate or dangerous. The controls decide which version you have.

Direction: a defensible version has a specific business purpose, institution-level invitation, reasonable travel class, no companions, no cash per diems, direct vendor payments, no tourism add-ons, and documentation before approval.

Do-not list:

- Do not wire travel funds to personal accounts.
- Do not add free days, family travel, or unapproved side trips.
- Do not let sales choose luxury treatment to protect the relationship.
- Do not approve after the travel has already happened unless escalated as a control failure.

Decisive questions:

- Why must this person physically attend?
- Was the institution invited, or the individual personally?
- What pending decision can the attendee influence?
- How will the expense be paid and recorded?

If-then triggers:

- If cash per diems or companions appear, stop and redesign or decline.
- If recipient-side rules are stricter than company rules, follow the stricter rule.
- If the business purpose cannot be written clearly, the trip should not be approved.

Trap named: incrementalism.

Expert routes: ABAC; Travel / expense controls; Governance; Business Expectations.

Program stress points: Standards and policies; Procedures and controls; Training and communication; Monitoring, auditing, and testing.

### Card 05 - Donation Or Sponsorship Near A Decision-Maker

Mode: M2 Judgment Call, possible M5 if the request appears tied to an official act.

Pattern: sponsorship, donation, foundation support, club support, academy support, or community project connected to a customer, official, federation, or decision-maker.

Opening move: A good cause with a powerful patron can be both real and risky. Treat it as an indirect-benefit question, not a charity question.

Direction: focus on who asked, who benefits, what is pending, how funds are governed, and whether the support is public and institution-level. Defensible support is documented, transparent, proportionate, paid to a legitimate entity, and separated from pending decisions.

Do-not list:

- Do not treat the charitable purpose as a risk cure.
- Do not route through an official's suggestion without enhanced review.
- Do not book a donation as marketing to avoid controls.
- Do not approve inside a tender or permit window without escalation.

Decisive questions:

- Who first proposed the donation or sponsorship?
- What does the patron personally gain: credit, photos, influence, board role, political capital?
- What decisions involving the company does the patron touch this year?
- Does the recipient have governance, accounts, and a real public purpose?

If-then triggers:

- If proposed by the official or customer decision-maker, enhanced review and default defer.
- If the recipient lacks basic transparency, consider in-kind support or decline.
- If timing overlaps with a pending decision, treat timing as the decisive fact.

Trap named: halo effect.

Expert routes: ABAC; Third Party Due Diligence; Governance; Finance controls.

Program stress points: Risk assessment; Standards and policies; Procedures and controls; Third-party risk management; Monitoring, auditing, and testing.

### Card 06 - Success Fee For An Intermediary

Mode: M2 Judgment Call, possible M5 where public decision influence or concealed services appear.

Pattern: agent, consultant, introducer, lobbyist, distributor, or adviser asks for a percentage success fee.

Opening move: A success fee is a question wearing a number. The number matters, but what success depends on matters more.

Direction: test what success means, who can influence it, what work product proves the service, and whether the fee is proportionate to actual work. Government-touching success plus percentage compensation is a high-risk structure and needs enhanced review.

Do-not list:

- Do not benchmark against what the agent asked for.
- Do not accept "that's how it works there" as analysis.
- Do not split fees into labels that hide the same total economics.
- Do not issue written comfort that functions as approval without conditions.

Decisive questions:

- Success equals whose signature or decision?
- What specific deliverables will exist as evidence of services?
- Why percentage compensation rather than milestone or work-based fees?
- Who recommended the intermediary?

If-then triggers:

- If success turns on a public, state-owned, federation, or government-facing decision, enhanced review regardless of percentage.
- If no work product can be described, stop: the fee may be buying access or influence.
- If a customer or official recommended the intermediary, treat that as a separate red flag.

Trap named: anchoring.

Expert routes: ABAC; Third Party Due Diligence; Governance / Tone at the Top; Contracts; Finance controls.

Program stress points: Risk assessment; Standards and policies; Procedures and controls; Third-party risk management; Monitoring, auditing, and testing.

### Card 07 - Offshore Or Third-Party Payment Request

Mode: M2 Judgment Call, possible M5 if linked to sanctions, bank holds, concealment, tax evasion, or ongoing misconduct.

Pattern: counterparty asks to be paid in another country, to a related company, to an individual, or through a different entity than the contracting party.

Opening move: Payment geometry question. Money should follow the contract, or the contract follows the money into trouble.

Direction: default no to payee, country, or account changes without written rationale, contract alignment, tax / AML review, beneficial ownership clarity, and finance-control approval. Legitimate treasury exceptions exist, but they are not urgent and not informal.

Do-not list:

- Do not process "just this once."
- Do not let Finance own the call alone.
- Do not accept same-group claims without ownership evidence.
- Do not route around a bank, sanctions, or compliance hold.

Decisive questions:

- Why is the contracting party not being paid directly?
- Does the alternative payee appear in the contract?
- Who owns and controls the alternative payee?
- Is urgency part of the request?

If-then triggers:

- If urgency plus opacity appears, stop and escalate.
- If the request follows a bank hold, treat as evasion-risk pattern.
- If tax-driven, require written tax and legal rationale from the counterparty and review internally.

Trap named: helpfulness bias.

Expert routes: ABAC; Sanctions; Third Party Due Diligence; Finance controls; Legal / external counsel.

Program stress points: Procedures and controls; Third-party risk management; Monitoring, auditing, and testing; Investigations and response.

### Card 08 - Vague Services Contract

Mode: M2 Judgment Call.

Pattern: contract describes services as strategic advisory, consulting, market access, government relations, relationship management, introductions, or similar vague labels.

Opening move: If you cannot tell from the contract what you would sue them for not delivering, the description is too vague.

Direction: rewrite until deliverables, verification method, service period, fees, evidence, and business rationale are specific. Disclaimers do not fix vague services.

Do-not list:

- Do not fix vagueness with a stronger anti-bribery clause alone.
- Do not sign now and promise to tighten later.
- Do not accept elegant senior-sounding language as proof of service.
- Do not let the lack of deliverables survive because the deal is urgent.

Decisive questions:

- What document, report, meeting log, analysis, or evidence lands in the file each period?
- Could an auditor reconstruct what was bought from the contract and invoices?
- Why does the counterparty resist specificity?
- Do services touch officials, state-owned entities, tenders, permits, or public decisions?

If-then triggers:

- If deliverables cannot be enumerated, stop or rewrite.
- If vague services touch public decision-making, enhanced ABAC / third-party review.
- If invoices mirror the vague wording, payment should be blocked until evidence exists.

Trap named: sophistication mimicry.

Expert routes: ABAC; Third Party Due Diligence; Contracts; Finance controls; Business Expectations.

Program stress points: Standards and policies; Procedures and controls; Third-party risk management; Monitoring, auditing, and testing.

### Card 09 - Discounts, Rebates, Credits, Or Free Inventory As Hidden Benefits

Mode: M2 Judgment Call.

Pattern: discount, rebate, credit note, free inventory, free tickets, marketing support, or commercial benefit may flow to an individual or decision-maker.

Opening move: Value is value. The label matters less than who receives the benefit and why.

Direction: trace the true beneficiary, business rationale, comparability, approval trail, and accounting treatment. If the benefit is person-directed or off-books, use the gift / hospitality / ABAC framework, not just pricing logic.

Do-not list:

- Do not let commercial teams classify the benefit alone.
- Do not hide benefits through netting or creative accounting.
- Do not treat free tickets as pricing if they benefit a person.
- Do not rely on invoice labels if the economic reality is different.

Decisive questions:

- Who is the end beneficiary: entity or individual?
- Would the same benefit be defensible for comparable customers?
- Is the benefit recorded visibly and accurately?
- Is there a pending decision by the beneficiary or their organization?

If-then triggers:

- If person-directed at a decision-maker, route through gifts / hospitality / ABAC controls.
- If Finance cannot book it transparently, it should not happen.
- If the benefit compensates for something not in the contract, escalate.

Trap named: category laundering.

Expert routes: ABAC; Finance controls; Business Expectations; Third Party Due Diligence.

Program stress points: Standards and policies; Procedures and controls; Training and communication; Monitoring, auditing, and testing.

### Card 10 - Relative-Owned Supplier Or Conflict

Mode: M2 Judgment Call, possible M5 if concealment, retaliation, senior involvement, or tainted decisions appear.

Pattern: employee, executive, or decision-maker has a family, ownership, friendship, financial, or outside-role link to supplier, customer, tender, or counterparty.

Opening move: A conflict disclosed early is a control problem. A conflict discovered later may be an integrity problem. Which one is this?

Direction: manage the conflict without moralizing it. Remove the conflicted person from the decision path, document the wall, rerun affected decisions if needed, and check whether terms changed while the conflict existed.

Do-not list:

- Do not punish honest disclosure in a way that teaches others to hide.
- Do not leave the wall informal.
- Do not let the conflicted person explain why the conflict is harmless.
- Do not ignore prior decisions already touched by the conflicted person.

Decisive questions:

- Was it disclosed proactively or discovered by someone else?
- What decisions did the conflicted person already influence?
- Are the supplier or customer terms at market?
- Is anyone senior enough to require independent oversight?

If-then triggers:

- If discovered late and decisions were touched, trigger a review of those decisions.
- If senior management is involved, consider independent supervision.
- If retaliation risk appears, route through speak-up / investigation controls.

Trap named: severity inversion.

Expert routes: Conflicts of Interest; ABAC; Third Party Due Diligence; Speak-Up; Investigations; Governance.

Program stress points: Governance and mandate; Standards and policies; Procedures and controls; Speak-up and reporting; Investigations and response; Incentives and discipline.

### Card 11 - Employee Data Review Across Jurisdictions

Mode: M3 Counsel-Boundary.

Pattern: compliance wants to review employee email, Teams, WhatsApp, laptop, phone, drive, logs, or other employee data across countries.

Opening move: This is a question I make better, not one I answer. Reviewability is jurisdiction-specific. A confident general answer would be unsafe.

Direction: Compliance defines the purpose, scope, necessity, proportionality, least-intrusive sequence, and preservation need. Counsel owns local lawfulness, works council, DPO, notice, privilege, transfer, and admissibility issues. The product output should be a counsel-ready review protocol, not a yes/no answer.

Do-not list:

- Do not start an informal search because IT technically can.
- Do not pull private-device or chat data without counsel.
- Do not expand from corporate email to broader sources without a reviewed rationale.
- Do not mix preservation with review authorization.

Decisive questions for counsel:

- What legal basis applies to each data category and country?
- Are notice, consultation, works council, DPO, or regulator steps required?
- Must data stay local, or can it be transferred?
- What sequencing preserves privilege-intended handling without over-claiming privilege?

If-then triggers:

- If review already started informally, stop, log what happened, and put counsel in control.
- If WhatsApp, private devices, or special-category data are in scope, assume the process is harder and slower.
- If there is immediate deletion risk, preserve first through lawful channels.

Trap named: tool availability bias.

Expert routes: Data / Privacy; Investigations; Legal / external counsel; HR; IT security.

Program stress points: Governance and mandate; Procedures and controls; Speak-up and reporting; Investigations and response; Monitoring, auditing, and testing.

### Card 12 - Whistleblower Statutory Clocks

Mode: M3 Counsel-Boundary.

Pattern: reporter feedback deadline, acknowledgement deadline, anonymous reporter handling, confidentiality, or what can be said to reporter.

Opening move: Two clocks and a confidentiality ceiling. The structure is predictable; exact duties are local-law questions.

Direction: Compliance owns process discipline: log day zero, calendar acknowledgement and follow-up deadlines, protect confidentiality, avoid outcome promises, and prepare process-language updates. Counsel confirms exact local deadlines, content, reporter rights, accused rights, and external-reporting consequences.

Do-not list:

- Do not promise the reporter a result.
- Do not disclose names, discipline, or investigation details to appear responsive.
- Do not let a deadline force oversharing.
- Do not treat anonymous reporting mechanics as an afterthought.

Decisive questions for counsel:

- What are the exact local acknowledgement and feedback deadlines?
- What can be said if the investigation is ongoing?
- What rights does the accused have regarding privacy and process?
- How are anonymous reporters updated in this channel?

If-then triggers:

- If the follow-up deadline arrives mid-investigation, provide process-language update rather than silence.
- If the reporter goes external or alleges retaliation, same-day escalation.
- If the report identifies senior management, mode may shift to M5.

Trap named: responsiveness bias.

Expert routes: Speak-Up; Investigations; Legal / external counsel; HR; Data / Privacy.

Program stress points: Speak-up and reporting; Investigations and response; Procedures and controls; Training and communication; Remediation and continuous improvement.

### Card 13 - Program Effective Despite Misconduct

Mode: M4 Program Self-Assessment, possible M3 if prepared for enforcement or litigation.

Pattern: board, management, auditor, regulator, or compliance leader asks whether the program was effective even though misconduct occurred.

Opening move: Misconduct does not automatically prove the program failed. The better question is what the program did before, during, and after.

Direction: build a gap map, not a verdict. Assess design, operation, detection, response, discipline, remediation, and learning. A usable answer may be mixed: strong detection, weak risk-to-control mapping, slow remediation, or unclear ownership.

Do-not list:

- Do not write the conclusion before the gap map.
- Do not confuse training completion with effectiveness.
- Do not ignore whether the risk was known and controlled.
- Do not let management appetite rewrite evidence.

Decisive questions:

- Was this risk identified in the risk assessment?
- Was a control assigned and actually operating?
- Who detected the issue and how fast?
- What changed after detection?
- Can evidence show operation, not only design?

If-then triggers:

- If detection came from regulator, media, or external audit, explain why internal detection failed and what changed.
- If this is for enforcement, litigation, or disclosure, counsel owns framing.
- If no control mapped to the risk, remediation must start there.

Trap named: defensive certainty.

Expert routes: Compliance Program; Monitoring; Investigations; Governance; Training; Remediation.

Program stress points: all 12 program elements may be in scope.

### Card 14 - Self-Reporting Decision

Mode: M5 Escalation-Mandatory.

Pattern: question asks whether to voluntarily disclose, self-report, notify regulator, report to tender authority, federation, law enforcement, enforcement agency, bank, or public body.

Opening move: This tool should not decide self-reporting. It can make you prepared for the room where counsel and leadership decide.

Direction: disclosure decisions require counsel and leadership under a controlled process. Compliance can preserve the record, freeze the timeline, identify regimes that may apply, document known / unknown facts at high level, and prepare a decision memo structure. It must not produce the recommendation in no-secret mode.

Do-not list:

- Do not type detailed case facts into an unapproved tool.
- Do not frame it as a simple disclose / not disclose binary before counsel maps regimes.
- Do not let time pass unlogged.
- Do not continue the underlying conduct while debating disclosure.

Decisive questions for the controlled process:

- When did the company know, who knew, and what did they know?
- Is the conduct continuing?
- Which regulators, authorities, contracts, banks, or tender rules could be implicated?
- What evidence is preserved?
- Who owns the decision and who is conflicted?

If-then triggers:

- If cooperation-credit windows may apply, counsel this week, not this quarter.
- If conduct may be continuing, stopping / preserving outranks the disclosure question.
- If senior management is implicated, independent supervision is required.

Trap named: deliberation drift.

Expert routes: Legal / external counsel; Governance; Investigations; Remediation; Board / Audit Committee.

Program stress points: Governance and mandate; Investigations and response; Monitoring, auditing, and testing; Remediation and continuous improvement.

### Card 15 - Allegation Against A Senior Executive

Mode: M5 Escalation-Mandatory, often with M1 if retaliation, evidence, or authority contact is immediate.

Pattern: allegation concerns CEO, board member, GC, CCO, senior executive, controlling shareholder, or a person who influences the compliance officer's role, budget, bonus, or reporting line.

Opening move: The accused person's reporting line cannot supervise the investigation. Structure first, facts second.

Direction: supervision must sit above and outside the accused's authority. For a CEO, that may mean board chair, audit committee, or independent directors. For a board member, independent board process. If the GC is accused, external counsel may need to report directly to the board or independent owner. The exact governance path depends on company documents and local law, but independence is not optional.

Do-not list:

- Do not inform the accused before preservation and independent supervision exist.
- Do not run quiet preliminary checks that tip off the person.
- Do not let loyalty, fear, or authority compress the structure.
- Do not ignore your own conflict if the accused controls your role.

Decisive questions:

- Who does the accused not control?
- Is the compliance officer's reporting line or job security affected?
- Is evidence within the accused's reach?
- Do board, D&O, investigation, or speak-up protocols prescribe a path?

If-then triggers:

- If the accused controls your role, budget, bonus, or reporting line, escalate through independent board / external counsel channel.
- If evidence could be altered, preservation precedes interviews or notification.
- If retaliation risk exists, protection measures start immediately.

Trap named: authority gradient at maximum pressure.

Expert routes: Investigations; Speak-Up; Governance; Legal / external counsel; HR; Board / Audit Committee.

Program stress points: Governance and mandate; Speak-up and reporting; Investigations and response; Incentives and discipline; Remediation and continuous improvement.
