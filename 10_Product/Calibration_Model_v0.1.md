# Calibration Model v0.1.1

Source: Tom's 2026-07-04 calibration note on "Legal Counsel wearing Compliance Hat."

## Status

This file captures how ACI-OS should calibrate its answer style and product experience to the user's actual compliance maturity and role context.

It is product strategy and behavior guidance, not a source-backed expert route.

## Core Insight

One of the strongest target groups is the Legal Counsel wearing the Compliance Hat.

This user is not unintelligent and not professionally junior. They may be a strong lawyer, HR leader, finance manager, risk manager, or internal control owner. The problem is that the company has suddenly made them responsible for compliance-program operation.

They may know law, contracts, HR process, finance controls, or governance. But they may panic when asked to own:

- Speak-up.
- Monitoring.
- Risk assessment.
- Tone at the top.
- Third-party management.
- Training.
- Investigations.
- Remediation.
- Board reporting.
- Policy lifecycle.
- Compliance calendar and annual plan.

Product implication: the app should not talk down to them. It should translate compliance-program operation into practical steps, decision rights, system logic, and first priorities.

A mature answer should be profile-aware. The user should feel the difference when ACI-OS can say: "Based on your company profile..."

## Persona: Legal Counsel Wearing Compliance Hat

Typical trigger:

The company says: "You are now Compliance Officer as well."

Typical emotional state:

- Capable, but anxious.
- Responsible, but unsure where to start.
- Used to legal analysis, but not always used to running an operating program.
- Afraid of missing a hidden compliance obligation.
- Pulled between business urgency, management expectations, HR sensitivity, legal privilege, and incomplete systems.

Typical knowledge profile:

- Stronger: legal risk, contracts, governance, privilege, negotiation, management advice.
- Weaker: compliance operating model, risk assessment, monitoring, speak-up triage, investigation protocol, training effectiveness, third-party lifecycle, root-cause remediation, board evidence, compliance KPIs.

## Calibration Questions

When the user profile is unknown, the product should infer or ask lightly:

1. Is your role mainly Compliance, Legal, HR, Finance, Risk, Internal Audit, Data Protection, or mixed?
2. Are you building the compliance program, running an existing one, or handling a specific incident?
3. How mature is the current setup: informal, basic, documented, tested, or mature?
4. Do you have dedicated Compliance resources or is this an added responsibility?
5. Which areas worry you most now: speak-up, investigations, third parties, sanctions, ABAC, monitoring, training, board reporting, or management pressure?

Do not ask all five every time. Use the smallest useful set.

## Answer Style For This Persona

For Legal Counsel wearing Compliance Hat, answers should:

- Acknowledge the role transition without dramatizing it.
- Give operating structure before theory.
- Translate compliance language into decision rights, workflows, controls, records, and escalation.
- Explain unknown compliance concepts briefly, without basic lectures.
- Offer a minimum viable program path where relevant.
- Separate legal advice from compliance operating judgment.
- Make the Legal / Compliance hat distinction explicit where the same person may be carrying both roles.
- Show what Legal owns, what Compliance owns, what management owns, and what external counsel or specialist advisers must own.
- Identify what Legal should own, what Compliance should own, what HR should own, what Audit should test, and what management must decide.
- Give templates, checklists, and sequencing.

Avoid:

- Assuming the user already has a mature compliance function.
- Treating them like a beginner in business.
- Explaining compliance as an academic subject.
- Dumping a full compliance-program manual when they need the next step.

## Calibration Levels

### Level 1 - Expert Compliance Leader

Needs second opinion, challenge, escalation logic, board wording, prioritization, and confirmation that nothing material is missing.

### Level 2 - Small-Team Compliance Operator

Needs what-to-do-first guidance, triage questions, red flags, templates, and management communication.

### Level 3 - Legal Counsel Wearing Compliance Hat

Needs operating-program translation: how to run speak-up, risk assessment, monitoring, third parties, training, investigations, reporting, and remediation without losing legal discipline.

### Level 4 - HR / Finance / Risk / Data / Audit Wearing Compliance Hat

Needs plain-language compliance architecture, ownership boundaries, escalation triggers, and minimum viable program guidance.

### Level 5 - Business Manager Facing Compliance Question

Needs clear practical do/don't guidance, escalation path, and why the control exists.

## Minimum Viable Program Path For The Persona

When the user says they have inherited compliance, the first path should be:

1. Mandate: who appointed you, what authority do you have, and where do you escalate?
2. Risk map: what are the top compliance risks by business model, country, third party, public-sector touchpoint, and transaction type?
3. Speak-up: is there a reporting channel, confidentiality process, anti-retaliation rule, and case owner?
4. Third parties: who approves agents, distributors, consultants, suppliers, JV partners, and intermediaries?
5. Sanctions and ABAC: what screening, ownership, payment, gifts, hospitality, donation, sponsorship, and public-official controls exist?
6. Investigations: who decides intake, preservation, privilege, interviews, findings, consequences, and remediation?
7. Training: who needs practical training for the real risks, not just annual e-learning?
8. Monitoring: what controls are tested and who reviews exceptions?
9. Reporting: what does management or the board need to know regularly?
10. Remediation: how are incidents converted into control fixes?

This is not a full program build. It is the first stabilization map.

## Product Implication

Calibration is not only industry, size, country, and risk appetite. It is also role identity and compliance maturity.

The same sanctions, ABAC, or speak-up scenario should sound different depending on whether the user is:

- A senior Group Head of Compliance.
- A small-team compliance operator.
- Legal Counsel suddenly wearing the Compliance Hat.
- HR or Finance inheriting compliance.
- A business manager asking whether they can proceed.

The underlying guardrails remain the same. The explanation layer, vocabulary, assumptions, and templates should adapt.

## Legal / Compliance Hat Split

For this persona, ACI-OS should often say the split explicitly:

- Legal hat: legal interpretation, privilege, statutory process, employment law, regulator-response position, contract enforceability, and legal advice.
- Compliance hat: operating model, controls, evidence, escalation, training, monitoring, third-party lifecycle, speak-up discipline, investigation protocol, remediation, and management reporting.
- Management hat: commercial decision, risk acceptance, mandate, resources, accountability, and tone.

This is not a hierarchy. It is an operating distinction. The same person may need to prepare all three views, but the record should not confuse them.
