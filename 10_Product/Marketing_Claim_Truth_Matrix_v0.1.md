# Marketing Claim Truth Matrix v0.1

Status: internal claim-to-earn discipline. Not public marketing copy.

Source: founder marketing-message canon, reviewed 2026-07-04.

## Purpose

This file converts future marketing lines into product truth requirements.

The rule is simple: ACI-OS should not say externally what the product cannot already demonstrate through behavior, artifacts, testing evidence, governance, or founder-verified facts.

Marketing should follow product truth, not lead it.

## Claim-To-Earn Rules

1. No external marketing, customer testing, investor posture, or public claim before external-exposure preconditions are checked.
2. Every claim must map to at least one product behavior, artifact, test, governance rule, or verified founder fact.
3. Avoid absolutes such as always, never, every, published, secure, privileged, or zero unless literally true and tested.
4. Boundary lines must travel with provocative lines. If the tagline implies first-call advice, the legal-advice and escalation boundary must appear nearby.
5. Security, hosting, retention, DPA, no-training, subprocessor, privilege, and procurement claims require technical architecture and legal review before use.
6. Founder biography claims require exact years, titles, employment/IP clearance, and side-activity clearance before use.
7. QA transparency claims require real logs and test evidence, not only a taxonomy.

## Build Implications

Near-term product work that makes the future lines true:

1. Add first-answer length / 10-second opening / 30-second readability checks.
2. Keep triage questions focused but do not promise exactly three unless the product is changed.
3. Add Reasoning Record export tests for serious matters.
4. Maintain and populate an internal Failure Log.
5. Add ABAC and investigations routes with escalation-vs-judgment tests.
6. Keep pattern-first behavior visible in no-secret mode.
7. Verify founder-year and title claims before any public use.
8. Keep tagline and legal-advice boundary inseparable.
9. For "Think Like Compliance," collect examples where the product changes a yes/no question into lifecycle, owner, control, evidence, documentation, training, monitoring, or escalation logic.
