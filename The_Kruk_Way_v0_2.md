## PART 1 — NEW DOCTRINES AND EXTENSIONS (delta over v0.1)

**D13 — The Code of Conduct is the roof, introduced by trigger, structured in 13 sections.**
A Code becomes necessary at identifiable trigger points: (1) "too big, too fast" — growth makes informal rules fail and policies pile up without hierarchy; (2) the multicultural workforce — the Code as "cultural glue," one set of rules a global organization can share; (3+) regulatory pressure, scandal, M&A. Function: it establishes the HIERARCHY of all internal rules (which procedure wins, what is expired, where employees find guidance). The 13-section reference structure (CEO message → values → who must follow → laws & policies → business integrity → stakeholder interactions [vertical] → digital ethics/AI/privacy → R&D ethics [vertical] → human rights & workplace → supply chain → CSR/ESG → whistleblowing → monitoring & enforcement) is a reusable template. *New pattern: CODE OF CONDUCT how-to. Elements: E3+E1.*

**D14 — The 13-element practitioner crosswalk is the Foundation Map's ancestor.**
Tom's own course table maps 13 practitioner elements (FCPA "Hallmarks" naming) against DOJ ECCP 2024 × ISO 37301 × USSG §8B2.1 × OIG × UKBA six principles, cell by cell, with section citations. This is the source of the "12-element practitioner model" resolved in Gate 1 Annex A — and independent proof that the crosswalk method (not preference) governs model decisions in the Kruk Way. *Action: attach as Annex A source artifact; GPT knowledge file (already partially in Foundation Map).*

**D15 — The five oversight instruments are different tools, not synonyms.**
Monitoring / review-audit / control / investigation / whistleblowing-reporting distinguished across five dimensions: timing, common perception, official definition, WHAT IT IS REALLY FOR, and the organization's stance. Key practitioner insights: an investigation is the chance for REVOLUTIONARY (fast) change of structure and culture, where monitoring and audit produce only evolutionary change; whistleblowing done right is "painless vigilance" (bezbolesne czuwanie). Officers should pick the instrument by the change they need, not by the calendar. *New pattern: OVERSIGHT INSTRUMENT selection. Elements: E6+E7.*

**D16 — Human-nature laws are compliance-design constraints.**
The program designer plans around: Murphy (what can go wrong will, at the worst moment → crisis headers exist), Parkinson (tasks fill the time given → deadlines in controls), Peter Principle (people rise to incompetence → competence checks in key control roles), Pygmalion (expectations shape results → tone and trust design), Ockham (do not multiply entities → D4 policy filter, 3-not-33 slides), Pareto (20% of activities carry 80% of risk → risk-based everything). *Integration: behavioral-design section of Core Spec §8 gains this list as design rationale.*

**D17 — Practitioner Rule #0 (2026 addition): "If you are overworked, you will see nothing."**
Added by Tom at the top of the tips list in the 2026 deck — the officer's own capacity is a detection control; an overloaded officer misses the signals the whole program exists to catch. Ties to the product thesis itself (the under-resourced officer) and to Mirror mode. *Phrase bank + Mirror-mode content.*

**Extensions to existing doctrines:**
- **D7 (Risk assessment)** gains the teaching-question technique: "PYTANKO" reality-anchored prompts ("How do YOU show tone in the middle in your team?" / "Which process in YOUR country carries the highest corruption risk?") — the question bank's pedagogical ancestor.
- **D8 (DD ladder)** gains the operational depth from W2: 3-tier risk classification (high = government/HCP contact, high-risk jurisdiction, flags; medium; low), EDD document set (UBO >25% disclosure, 3-year financials, multi-list screening, signed ABAC declaration, COI disclosure, adverse media), cumulative contract clauses, onboarding (training + BANK ACCOUNT VERIFICATION at vendor registration — the TC5 control, found in Tom's own procedure), monitoring (transaction monitoring, annual DD refresh, surprise audits for high-risk).
- **D12 (Geopolitics)** gains the sanctions-practice layer from the Russia deck: the "owner of everything" problem (who owns the distributor, the bank, the office building, the ISP, the leased cars — designation checks reach infrastructure, not only counterparties); continuous re-screening as workload ("is one check enough? new owners, new sanctions daily"); payments to accounts AT sanctioned banks as a separate prohibition channel; evasion typologies (family members, real estate, complex ownership, third-country jurisdictions, false trade information — REPO task force patterns); and the multi-regime map including Ukrainian lists as a distinct layer (TC7's origin) plus sector nuances (e.g., pharma General Licenses making US narrower than EU).
