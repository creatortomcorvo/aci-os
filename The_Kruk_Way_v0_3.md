## PART 1 — NEW DOCTRINES (delta over v0.2)

**D18 — Every program element communicates in three cost tiers, with a must-have floor and an evidence trail.**
The communication method: for EACH of the seven program elements, plan communication in three tiers — low/no-cost "easy wins" (intranet, one-page briefs, town-hall minutes, QR-code posters, recognition shoutouts) · medium-cost (e-learning, scenario videos, champions programs, escape-room training, helpdesks, AI chatbots) · high-cost strategic (mobile apps, leadership summits, external certification, VR/AR simulation). Each element also carries a **minimum requirement (must-have)** and — critically — **evidence-collection methods** (signed acknowledgments, access logs, attendance records, meeting minutes, survey results): every communication measure is paired with the proof it happened, which is exactly what an ECCP review asks for. Plus the coordination checklist repeated on every slide: *Should I handle this alone? Who can assist? Who is already doing this? Which function has similar needs? Do we have a training department?* — the invisible-program doctrine (D5) operationalized.
*New pattern: COMMUNICATION PLANNING how-to (element × cost tier × must-have × evidence). Elements: E4+E6. Includes the 12-month communication plan template (Jan kickoff/leadership → Feb policies → Mar training → Apr speak-up → May incentives → Jun role-specific → Jul crisis drills → Aug risk/monitoring → Sep board deep-dive → Oct advanced learning → Nov audit/surveys → Dec year-end review).*

**D19 — Compliance communication is a nervous system: clarity over quantity, never 100%.**
Three principles, verbatim doctrine: (1) compliance is one of ten or more supporting functions — *make your message matter without competing for attention*; (2) *unnecessary communication pollutes the message* — precision ensures engagement; (3) **"The Invisible Guide: compliance — like the nervous system — should be ever-present yet unobtrusive, felt in action, not in excess words."** And the closing rule on the 12-month plan: *"NEVER EVER implement 100% of these activities. Choose wisely and let people breathe."* The final exam question of the whole lecture: *who do you want to be for your organization — a police officer, or a partner?*
*Phrase bank (nervous-system line ★, breathe line ★) + proportionality doctrine extended to communication. → article candidate: "Your compliance program should feel like a nervous system, not a megaphone."*

**Extension to D13 (Code of Conduct):** the CoC Elements working note adds the practitioner element list (ethical decision-making strategy · COI/gifts/meals/entertainment · financial-records accuracy · clinical-trials & R&D ethics · human rights · equal opportunity · responsible procurement · EHS · healthcare laws · ABAC · AML · anti-slavery/modern-slavery · inside information · press & media relations · hotline & non-retaliation) plus one governance technicality worth keeping: the Code should reserve the company's right to modify the program and state that in a conflict, the currently effective specific policy governs — the hierarchy clause in practice (observed in the published Code of *a global biotech company*; name removed per sanitization rule).
